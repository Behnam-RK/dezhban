dezhban is installed. It is NOT enforcing anything yet.

Two steps to go. In Terminal:

  1. Configure it:     sudo dezhban setup
  2. Start it:         sudo dezhban start

Then open Dezhban from your Applications folder — it lives in the menubar, shows
what the kill switch is doing, and from there Block, Unblock and "Switching VPN…"
work with no password.

Check it any time with:

    dezhban status

The "daemon control" line tells you whether routine operations will ask for a
password. Once the service is running, they will not.

If anything goes wrong and you lose network access, this always restores it,
even with the service dead:

    sudo dezhban panic

To remove dezhban completely:

    sudo sh /usr/local/share/dezhban/uninstall.sh
